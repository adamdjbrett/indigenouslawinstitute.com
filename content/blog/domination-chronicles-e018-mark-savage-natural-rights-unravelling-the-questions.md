Attorney Mark Savage joins the show for a conversation on plenary power, natural rights, treaties, and original free existence.

For ILI readers, this conversation is useful because it connects litigation questions with deeper assumptions about rights and relations. Mark Savage helps the hosts revisit plenary power and ask what changes when original free existence is taken seriously.

This short resource post is part of an Indigenous Law Institute guide to the *Domination Chronicles* archive. The goal is to help readers move from ILI's work on empire, Christian discovery, federal Indian law, and the right of domination into the podcast conversations where those themes are examined episode by episode.
